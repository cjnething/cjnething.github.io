Software · Education · Research

Software engineer, educator, and researcher interested in human capability, learning, and AI.

Across my career and education, I've repeatedly been drawn to understanding how people learn, solve problems, and develop expertise. I build software, teach engineers, and design systems that help people become more capable—not just more engaged.

AI feels like a natural continuation of those interests, not a recent pivot. The question I've been pursuing for years is the same one that drew me toward coursework in Educational Technology, Human-Computer Interaction, and Knowledge-Based AI: how can technology help people think, learn, and do more?

I also follow curiosity into systems most people take for granted—how a nose turns molecules into information, why two color experts can disagree on the same person, and why a Sologub character might assume a houseplant is an assassination attempt. How my brain works →
